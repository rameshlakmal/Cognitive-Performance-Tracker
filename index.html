Literature Survey

Over the past few years, significant advancements have been made in the development of systems for tracking dementia, allowing for more accurate diagnosis and treatment of this debilitating disease. However, advances in technology have made it possible to develop a system for detection and track treatment processes for vascular dementia. Those systems use combination of imaging techniques, such as MRI and CT scans, to detect changes in the brain that are characteristic of the disease. It also employs cognitive tests to assess the patient's cognitive function and track any changes over time. Additionally, the system includes a treatment plan that may include medications, lifestyle changes, and other interventions to slow the progression of the disease. With 13 this system, healthcare providers can accurately diagnose vascular dementia and monitor the patient's progress to ensure they receive the most effective treatment possible.

Research Problem

Dementia is a disease that impairs the ability to remember, think, or make decisions and interferes with everyday activities. The second most typical form of dementia is vascular dementia. It is caused by issues with the blood flow to the brain. Vascular dementia can have a sudden onset, with symptoms appearing suddenly after a stroke or a series of small strokes. The progression of vascular dementia can be more variable than that of other diseases. In some cases, the symptoms of vascular dementia can improve with treatment, but in many other cases, the symptoms will persist and may worsen over time. The treatment of vascular dementia can vary depending on the underlying cause of blood vessel damage and may include medications, lifestyle changes, rehabilitation, and surgery. The diagnosis of vascular dementia can be more challenging than the diagnosis of other types of dementia diseases, as it requires a comprehensive evaluation of medical history, symptoms, and test results, including imaging tests and blood tests. The easiest way to identify if a person is normal or atypical is with a simple questionnaire. There are a few tests that are used, such as the Neuropsychiatry Unit Cognitive Assessment Tool (NUCAG) and the Mini-Mental State Exam (MMSE), that give patients a series of questions and tasks to determine their various cognitive functions. Most of them are still happening as a manual process. A doctor or a nurse would ask the questions and assign the tasks, and based on the patient's responses, they would assign a suitable grade. If this process were automated, the data collected could be used for a variety of other purposes. Also, each patient's efficient treatment process can be different from one to another, and finding out which one of them is which can be easier with those tests too..

Objectives

Main Objective:

Using the NUCOG assessment tool and other tests as reference, creating an accurate system for early detection and monitoring of vascular dementia treatment progress.

Sub Objective 1:

A dependable and accurate system is required to identify and track the risk of vascular dementia by analyzing the ability to follow simple commands.

Sub Objective 2:

Develop a reliable and accessible system for the analyzing and monitoring of dementia stages through speech analysis.

Sub Objective 3:

Develop a reliable and accessible system for the analyzing and monitoring of dementia stages through mathematical questions.

Sub Objective 4:

Develop a reliable and accessible system for the analyzing and monitoring through writing skills analysis.

Developing the model

This research belongs to the machine learning model. Therefore, the machine learning model training process has been followed throughout the methodology. Before developing the final model various types of models have been developed based on previous research to determine the best algorithm which provides higher rates of accuracy. When considering the disease identification component, image augmentation has been used to randomize the form of each image to provide better accuracy. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) have been used for the disease identification process. This model provides more accuracy than others. The model extracts feature on its own, thereby learning by itself to provide higher accuracy. CNN basically uses segments and layers of the original image, identifies key features aggregates them and derive at a partial prediction.
